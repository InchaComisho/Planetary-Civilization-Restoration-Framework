# Repository Map

This map organizes public InchaComisho repositories by theme and by type of work.

Labels used in this map:

- **Conceptual framework**: a structure for thinking, organizing, or integrating ideas.
- **Hypothesis**: a proposed explanation or possibility requiring further evidence.
- **Risk scenario**: a warning, failure mode, or collapse pathway.
- **Technical proposal**: a proposed system, mechanism, or implementation direction.
- **Governance proposal**: a proposal about decision-making, oversight, values, or safety rules.
- **Public communication**: explanatory or outreach material for broad readers.
- **Fiction / narrative**: story-based or speculative communication.

The classifications are navigational aids, not scientific validation.

## Uncertain Classifications

Some repositories may reasonably belong to multiple categories. For example:

- Direct cooling repositories often overlap with ocean, desert, and urban regeneration.
- Artificial Wisdom repositories overlap with civilization design and AI governance.
- Climate risk repositories may also function as public communication.
- Fiction/narrative repositories may communicate real risk scenarios without being technical proposals.

When in doubt, use the classification that helps a first-time reader find the repository safely and understand its limits.
