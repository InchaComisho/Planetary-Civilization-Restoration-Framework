## Abstract

**Planetary Civilization Restoration Framework** is a master index and navigation map for public repositories created by InchaComisho.

This repository is not a new technical proposal. It is an open archive index for ideas, hypotheses, conceptual frameworks, risk scenarios, technical proposals, governance proposals, public communication materials, and fiction/narrative works related to planetary restoration and civilization design.

The central purpose is to help first-time readers understand the whole framework without getting lost.

This index does not claim that every repository is scientifically proven. It organizes the repository ecosystem as an open integrated framework for direct cooling, carbon fixation recovery, and planetary circulation restoration.

---

## Why This Index Exists

The InchaComisho repository ecosystem spans climate risk, direct planetary cooling, ocean and water circulation, microbial and carbon fixation systems, ecosystem regeneration, Artificial Wisdom, civilization design, future computing, and public communication.

Without a master map, readers may encounter one repository and mistake it for the whole project. This index provides a cautious reading path and separates different types of work:

- Conceptual framework
- Hypothesis
- Risk scenario
- Technical proposal
- Governance proposal
- Public communication
- Fiction / narrative

---

## Core Thesis

The framework explores the hypothesis that emissions reduction is necessary but may not be sufficient by itself to restore planetary stability.

This index does **not** deny emissions reduction. Emissions reduction remains an essential layer of climate response.

The repositories mapped here explore a possible missing layer: direct cooling research, carbon fixation recovery, ecosystem regeneration, water circulation repair, and civilization-scale governance guided by Natural Law and Artificial Wisdom.

These materials should be read as a research and discussion framework, not as proof that any single intervention is ready, complete, or sufficient.

- independent scientific scrutiny

- reversibility and auditability
- legal and governance review
